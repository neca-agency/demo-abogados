# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective clients researching legal representation — individuals or small businesses facing a legal matter and evaluating whether this firm is trustworthy, competent, and a good fit. They arrive with uncertainty and often under stress; they need to quickly understand what the firm does, who they'll be working with, and how to take the next step without feeling pressured or talked down to.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio-quality marketing site for a law firm that demonstrates impeccable design craft while serving real user needs: build credibility, explain services clearly, and make contact feel natural. Success means a visitor leaves thinking "this firm is serious, human, and worth calling" — and the site itself is evidence of the care they'd receive.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative and warm. Expert confidence without coldness — the voice of a seasoned lawyer who explains clearly and listens first. Editorial restraint over salesmanship; credibility through craft and clarity, not legal clichés or urgency tactics.

## Anti-references

- Generic law-firm templates: stock gavels, scales of justice hero images, navy-and-gold palettes, rotating hero sliders
- AI-generated slop: cream/sand body backgrounds, gradient text, identical icon-card grids, eyebrow kickers on every section
- Aggressive sales patterns: pop-ups, "FREE CONSULTATION" banners, fake urgency, lead-capture before trust is established
- Cold corporate legal sites: sterile layouts, impersonal stock photography, walls of undifferentiated service copy

## Design Principles

1. **Expert confidence without intimidation** — Authority comes from clear language, structured information, and visual precision, not jargon or decorative legal symbolism.
2. **Editorial restraint** — Typography, whitespace, and hierarchy carry credibility. Every visual element earns its place; nothing decorates for decoration's sake.
3. **Show, don't sell** — Build trust through substance and craft. The site demonstrates the firm's attention to detail; it never begs for a click.
4. **Warmth in the details** — Human touches in copy, photography direction, and micro-interactions signal approachability without undermining professionalism.
5. **Clear path to contact** — Every key page answers two questions: "Can I trust them?" and "How do I reach them?" Contact is always findable, never ambushed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline: sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 body text), keyboard navigability, visible focus states, semantic HTML, and `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for any animation. Content should be readable on mobile-first viewports where most research happens.
